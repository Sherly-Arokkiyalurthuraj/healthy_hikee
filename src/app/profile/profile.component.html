Yoga

Achieve a fit and healthier lifestyle, along with a peaceful mind and happier life, by incorporating yoga into your daily routine. Don't be intimidated by complex asanas; you can practice simple poses and exercises that will keep you fit, whether you are overweight or underweight. Here are some simple asanas, along with their medicinal benefits, and you can click on the steps to learn how to perform them.

Tadasana

  • Improves posture, balance, and circulation.
  • Reduces stress and calms the mind.
  • Stand with feet together, heels slightly apart.
  • Engage your core and lengthen your spine.
  • Raise arms overhead, keeping them parallel.
  • Reach fingertips towards the ceiling while grounding feet.
  • Hold for 15-30 seconds, focusing on balance.
  • Exhale and lower arms back to sides.
Vrikshasana

  • Improves balance and coordination.
  • Strengthens core muscles and boosts confidence.
  • Stand in Mountain Pose.
  • Shift weight onto your left foot.
  • Place the sole of your right foot on your inner left thigh or calf.
  • Bring hands to chest or extend them overhead.
  • Hold for 15-30 seconds, maintaining balance.
  • Exhale and lower right leg. Repeat on the other side.
Bhujangasana

  • Strengthens the spine and stimulates abdominal organs.
  • Reduces stress and alleviates fatigue.
  • Lie on stomach with legs extended and feet apart.
  • Place palms under shoulders, elbows close to body.
  • Inhale and lift chest off the floor using back muscles.
  • Keep elbows slightly bent and shoulders away from ears.
  • Hold for 15-30 seconds.
  • Exhale and lower chest back to the floor.
Adho Mukha Svanasana

  • Stretches the whole body and strengthens muscles.
  • Improves digestion and relieves stress.
  • Start on hands and knees.
  • Tuck toes under and lift hips towards the ceiling.
  • Press hands into the mat, push heels towards the floor.
  • Engage core and lengthen spine.
  • Hold for 15-30 seconds.
  • Exhale and lower hips back to the starting position.
Paschimottanasana

  • Stretches the spine and hamstrings.
  • Improves digestion and relieves stress.
  • Sit with legs extended straight in front of you.
  • Inhale and lengthen your spine.
  • Exhale and fold forward from your hips, reaching for your feet or shins.
  • Keep your back as straight as possible and hold for 15-30 seconds.
  • Inhale and slowly come back to the seated position.
Savasana

  • Promotes deep relaxation.
  • Improves sleep and reduces blood pressure.
  • Lie flat on your back with legs extended and arms at your sides, palms facing up.
  • Close your eyes and relax your entire body. Focus on your breath.
  • Take slow, deep breaths and let go of any tension.
  • Stay in the pose for 5-10 minutes.
  • To exit, gently roll to one side and then slowly rise to a seated position.
